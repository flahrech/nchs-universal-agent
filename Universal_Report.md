## Executive Summary

Telehealth is increasingly recognized as a vital modality for delivering pediatric care, particularly for adolescents who benefit from its convenience, privacy, and accessibility. As Florida’s Medicaid and private payers align with national trends, the future of pediatric telehealth reimbursement looks poised for expansion but accompanied by critical policy considerations. This memo, grounded in comprehensive research from the Children’s Hospital of Philadelphia (CHOP) and Texas Children’s Hospital, outlines the current landscape, emerging reimbursement trends, and strategic recommendations for NCHS leadership to influence and adapt to the evolving environment.

---

## Background

Adolescents stand to gain uniquely from telehealth services due to their technological fluency, desire for confidentiality, and logistical barriers to in-person care. CHOP’s extensive research demonstrates high acceptability and feasibility of telehealth among adolescent patients and their families, with no compromise in quality when compared to in-person services across behavioral health, reproductive health, and chronic disease management domains.

Texas Children’s Hospital serves as a model in expanding telehealth access in rural and underserved pediatric populations. Their Medicaid policy enhancements—such as coverage of teleconsultations, school-based telemedicine, and remote monitoring—provide a valuable framework to anticipate reimbursement developments relevant to Florida.

---

## Current Challenges and Disparities

- **Access Inequities:** Telehealth utilization disparities exist among socio-economic and racial/ethnic groups, driven by differential broadband access and technology availability.  
- **Provider Readiness:** Varied provider enthusiasm and capability to integrate telehealth highlight needs for ongoing training and infrastructure support.  
- **Policy Gaps:** Florida’s specific Medicaid and private payer telehealth reimbursement policies are evolving but currently lack comprehensive coverage of all relevant modalities including audio-only and asynchronous services.

---

## Emerging Policy and Reimbursement Trends

### 1. **Comprehensive Coverage Expansion**  
Payers are expected to continue expanding telehealth reimbursement to include:  
- Audio-only visits, critical for populations with limited broadband.  
- Remote patient monitoring and caregiver training sessions.  
- School-based telehealth, enabling access where youth spend much of their time.

### 2. **Parity and Payment Models**  
Parity laws ensuring telehealth services reimburse at rates comparable to in-person visits promote provider participation and stabilize telehealth’s role in pediatric care.

### 3. **Cross-State Licensing and Flexible Supervision**  
Regulatory adaptations that facilitate cross-state practice and flexible supervision will expand the pediatric telehealth workforce, enhancing specialist availability in Florida.

### 4. **Equity-Driven Investments**  
Telehealth policies increasingly incorporate equity focus, investing in broadband infrastructure, technology access programs, and culturally competent care delivery models to reduce utilization disparities.

---

## Strategic Implications for NCHS

- **Advocacy for Stable Reimbursement**: Proactively engaging with Florida Medicaid and private payers to advocate for sustained, parity-based reimbursement for all pediatric telehealth modalities—including audio-only and remote caregiver training—will secure financial viability and access.  
- **Investment in Provider Infrastructure**: Supporting telehealth training programs and telehealth-tailored infrastructure for clinicians will improve care quality and provider readiness.  
- **Focus on Equity**: Implement targeted interventions to address digital divide issues affecting our patient populations, including partnerships to improve broadband access and culturally appropriate outreach.  
- **Data-Driven Policy Engagement**: Utilize NCHS patient data to demonstrate telehealth’s effectiveness and equity impact, informing public policy and payer decisions.  
- **Collaboration with Peer Institutions**: Leverage insights from CHOP and Texas Children’s Hospital to develop best practices and policy proposals tailored to Florida’s demographics and healthcare environment.

---

## Conclusion

Telehealth represents a transformative opportunity to expand pediatric care reach and quality in Florida, especially in adolescent populations. To realize its full potential, reimbursement policies must evolve to support flexible, comprehensive, and equitable telehealth services. Nicklaus Children's Health System is positioned to lead in shaping these policies through strategic advocacy, provider capacity building, and equity-focused initiatives. The integration of telehealth as a permanent, reimbursed component of pediatric healthcare will be essential to meeting the complex needs of Florida’s children now and in the future.

**Telehealth as a Promising Strategy in the Future of Adolescent Health — Children’s Hospital of Philadelphia (CHOP) PolicyLab Issue Brief**

*Date: 2024*

**Background:**
Adolescents are uniquely positioned to benefit from telehealth due to their tech-savviness, increasing independence, and need for privacy. However, prior data on telehealth for this group was limited. CHOP’s PolicyLab conducted multiple studies demonstrating strong interest and acceptability of telehealth among adolescent patients and their caregivers.

**Key Research Findings:**

- **Acceptability and Feasibility:**  
  Most adolescent patients and their families find telehealth care to be highly acceptable and feasible, providing convenience and reducing barriers to access without compromising perceived quality.

- **Quality of Care:**  
  Studies showed telehealth delivery yields care quality comparable to in-person visits for many adolescent health needs, including behavioral health, sexual and reproductive health, and chronic condition management.

- **Provider Perspectives:**  
  Providers generally find telehealth feasible to implement, though support varies by provider type and specialty. Adequate training and infrastructure are critical to sustain telehealth use.

- **Disparities:**  
  Despite overall positive outcomes, disparities remain in telehealth utilization linked to socio-economic status, broadband access, race/ethnicity, and language proficiency. These disparities risk widening health inequities if unaddressed.

- **Innovative Applications:**  
  Telehealth offers opportunities such as virtual group visits, caregiver training, and school-based telehealth programs, expanding access for underserved youth.

**Policy and Reimbursement Landscape:**

- **Reimbursement Needs:**  
  Sustaining telehealth beyond the public health emergency requires reliable reimbursement policies that cover audio-only telehealth, remote monitoring, and caregiver training.

- **Recommendation for Payers:**  
  Medicaid and private insurers should continue and expand coverage of telehealth services relevant to adolescent care, maintaining payment parity with in-person visits, and supporting flexible modalities including asynchronous and audio-only visits.

- **Licensing and Regulation:**  
  Policies facilitating cross-state licensing and supervision flexibility can enhance access and quality in pediatric telehealth, aligning with best clinical practices evolving during the COVID-19 pandemic.

- **Equity Focus:**  
  Telehealth policies must explicitly address and mitigate disparities by investing in broadband infrastructure, technology access programs, and culturally competent care models.

**Conclusion:**

Telehealth is a promising, feasible modality to increase adolescent health care access without sacrificing quality. For Florida’s pediatric population and beyond, policies that stabilize and expand telehealth reimbursement, address equity, and ensure provider readiness are crucial. CHOP’s research supports integrating telehealth as an essential complement to traditional care models in pediatric health settings.

---

**Texas Children's Hospital Pediatric Telemedicine Policy Highlights**

- Telemedicine is defined as electronic exchange of medical information to improve patient care and education.

- Telehealth services include teleconsultation (videoconference for specialist input), tele-education (provider education), and remote patient monitoring.

- Texas Children's initiatives emphasize expanding telehealth use in rural and underserved pediatric populations.

- Medicaid reimbursement policies in Texas have been updated to cover school-based pediatric telemedicine, teleconsults, and certain procedure codes (e.g., T1015).

- Future reimbursement strategy involves ongoing evaluation of telehealth service codes, provider types, and supervision models to support pediatric telehealth sustainability and access equity.

---

**Florida Pediatric Telehealth Reimbursement Outlook (Contextualized):**

While Florida-specific detailed reimbursement policies are evolving, the broader national CMS and Medicaid trends suggest:

- Continued expansion of telehealth coverage, including audio-only modalities, behavioral health, caregiver training.

- Non-facility Payment for home-based telehealth visits to maintain access during and beyond COVID-19 pandemic.

- Increased alignment between Medicare, Medicaid, and private payer telehealth reimbursement rules to reduce complexity for providers.

- State Medicaid programs like Florida’s are anticipated to follow the national trend to sustain telehealth, particularly for pediatric populations, guided by data and learnings from leading institutions like CHOP and Texas Children's.

---

This comprehensive view drawn from CHOP and Texas Children's research and policy materials forms the foundation for anticipating the future of pediatric telehealth reimbursement in Florida. It highlights the necessity for policies that stabilize telehealth use, ensure equitable access, cover diverse modalities, and support provider capacity building.

If Nicklaus Children's Health System leadership adopts these insights, they can better navigate policy changes and advocate for reimbursement frameworks that sustain and enhance pediatric telehealth services in Florida.
